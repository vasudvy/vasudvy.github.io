Problem Statement

Adults who lose speech ability after stroke or vocal cord surgery face significant communication barriers. Existing solutions provide poor speech quality and user experience, leading to social isolation and reduced quality of life.

Current Alternatives Primary Limitations
AAC Devices Hefty upfront cost
Electrolarynx Devices Robotic voice quality and social stigma
Surgical Interventions Expensive with 30% failure rates
Neck-mounted Devices Stream audio via speakers creating uncanny experience
Speech Therapy Natural-recovery has high dropout rates

Our Solution: Resonance

Neck patch prosthetic device

We are developing Resonance, a wearable device that users can apply as a patch on their neck. Using artificial intelligence, the device generates prosodic vibrations which are filtered into audible speech through the user's natural mouth and lip movements.

Key Advantages

  • Non-invasive: Requires no surgical intervention
  • Adaptive: Improves performance with continued usage
  • Natural: Produces interpretable speech for listeners
  • Accessible: Cost-effective alternative to existing solutions

Technical Insights and Innovation

AI Prosodic Generator

Our system employs a sophisticated AI prosodic generator that produces dynamic vibration patterns across the 80-300Hz frequency spectrum. The system intelligently matches prosody with phoneme characteristics and calibrates precise vibrational tones.

Frequency Range (Hz) Phoneme Classification Vibration Pattern
80-120 Vowels (a, e, i, o, u) Sustained resonance
120-200 Consonants (b, d, g, m, n) Pulsed modulation
200-300 Fricatives (f, s, sh, th) High-frequency bursts

Critical Clinical Discovery

Our research reveals that in post-surgical patients, muscular movement capacity is frequently preserved. This insight enables our system to detect and utilize existing muscle contractions to generate appropriate vibration patterns for speech reconstruction.

Preserved Physiological Functions

  • Laryngeal muscle contractions - Voluntary control retained
  • Diaphragmatic movement patterns - Breathing coordination intact
  • Oral articulation reflexes - Mouth and tongue mobility preserved
  • Neural pathway integrity - Motor control pathways functional
