Problem

Adults who lose speech ability after stroke or vocal cord surgery face significant communication barriers. Existing solutions provide poor speech quality and user experience, leading to social isolation and reduced quality of life.

  • Devices incur hefty upfront cost.
  • Surgeries are expensive with 30% failure rates
  • Neck-mounted devices stream audio via speakers from neck creating uncanny experience.

Solution



Neck patch prosthetic device

We are creating Resonance a device which user can wear as patch on neck and using AI the device generates prosodic vibrations which are filtered out as audible speech through user's natural mouth & lip movement.

  • Non invasive: requires no surgery
  • Adaptive: gets better with usage
  • Audible: interpretable speech for end-listener

How it Works

1. Sensors capture laryngeal muscle signals
↓
2. Trained ML model translates signals to phoneme patterns
↓
3. AI generates prosodic vibrations delivered by actuator
↓
4. Electromechanical force applied to user's face
↓
5. Mouth and lip movements filter vibrations as speech
with real-time prosodic adjustment

Insights

AI Prosodic Generator

We are training an AI prosodic generator that generates dynamic vibration movements across 80-300Hz spectrum. It matches prosody with phoneme and sets up exact tone for vibration.

Frequency Range Phoneme Type Vibration Pattern
80-120Hz Vowels (a, e, i, o, u) Sustained resonance
120-200Hz Consonants (b, d, g, m, n) Pulsed modulation
200-300Hz Fricatives (f, s, sh, th) High-frequency bursts

Key Clinical Insight

In post-surgical patients, muscle movement is not lost. This means we can track muscle movements to create necessary vibration patterns for speech reconstruction.

Preserved Functions:
  • Laryngeal muscle contractions
  • Diaphragmatic movement patterns
  • Oral articulation reflexes
  • Neural pathway integrity
